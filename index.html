Adolf Hitler

1889 - 1945

Dictator of Nazi Germany whose actions led to World War II and the Holocaust. This documentary examines one of history's darkest chapters to ensure we remember and learn from the past.

Biography

Adolf Hitler was born on April 20, 1889, in Braunau am Inn, Austria-Hungary. He was the fourth of six children born to Alois Hitler and Klara Pölzl. His early life was marked by mediocrity in school and a failed attempt to become an artist.

After serving in World War I, Hitler became involved in politics and joined the German Workers' Party in 1919, which later became the National Socialist German Workers' Party (Nazi Party). He became its leader in 1921.

His rise to power culminated in his appointment as Chancellor of Germany in January 1933. He quickly established a totalitarian dictatorship, dismantled democracy, and began implementing his racist ideology.

Hitler's aggressive foreign policy led to the invasion of Poland in 1939, starting World War II. His regime orchestrated the Holocaust, resulting in the genocide of six million Jews and millions of others including Roma, disabled persons, political opponents, and others deemed "undesirable."

Why We Remember

The study of Adolf Hitler and the Nazi regime serves as a critical reminder of the dangers of totalitarianism, racism, and unchecked power. The Holocaust represents one of humanity's darkest chapters, demonstrating how ideology, propaganda, and dehumanization can lead to unspeakable atrocities.

World War II reshaped the global order, led to the creation of the United Nations, and established new frameworks for international law and human rights. The Nuremberg Trials set precedents for prosecuting war crimes and crimes against humanity.

By studying this history, we honor the victims, understand the mechanisms that allowed such horrors to occur, and commit ourselves to preventing such tragedies in the future. As philosopher George Santayana wrote: "Those who cannot remember the past are condemned to repeat it."
